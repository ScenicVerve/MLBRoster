MLB Players 1901-2013

Jovita L De Loatch
February 20, 2015

Motivation

  • Mark Armour and Dan Levitt, for the Society for American Baseball Research numerically defined the spread of integration in major league baseball in the years following Jackie Robinsons 1947 debut (thus breaking the color barrier for African-Americans and Afro-Latinos).

  • They show that after steady participation (1972-1996) between 16%-19%, it has since plummeted by more than half. They concluded the decline has been exacerbated by the increased share of roster spots going to pitchers and catchers" who tend to be uncommon among African-Americans. [1]

  • What other trends can baseball player data show? Are there other metrics that can quantify baseball's evoling demographics? Here's a tool to help.

The App

  • This is a tool to explore baseball team rosters and to reveal general trends (future development could include full dynamic map update, time series(w/dates & stints), reactive intergation with table search and more sophisicated algorithms and analysis).
  • The App currently plots the hometown of each the players in the National League batters and allows users to query the roster over the periods of 1901 to 2013. In addition, some summary plots are presented behind the tab.

Lahman Baseball Database

  • I added geocodes(Google Map API) to data from Sean Lahman's Baseball Database [2], starting at the Dead Ball Era (1901 - 1920) or sometimes called the Segregation Era (1901 - 1947ish)

when Jackie Robinson broke the color line in 1947 to Lahman current R package upload to 2013.

  • While this tool can't look at race, it does provide mechanisms to explore the growing world of baseball. If Negro Baseball Leagues and international leagues data becomes available, a more informed look at diversification and internationalization of Baseball can be made.

Data Criteria

  • A broad selection criteria used based on pension minimum service of 43 days in the majors (2011) annual pension plan.
